Game Rules & FAQ
Blinds & Minimum Raises
Forced Blinds ($10 / $20)
Before cards drop, the Dealer automatically posts the $10 Small Blind, and the opponent posts the $20 Big Blind. Money goes in *before* seeing cards. You can fold post-deal, but your blind is forfeit.
The Minimum Raise Rule
A raise must match the current high bet PLUS increase it by at least the amount of the previous bet/raise increment. Pre-flop, since the Big Blind is $20, the first legal minimum raise size is $40 total.
Hand Rankings
1. Royal Flush
A, K, Q, J, 10 of the exact same suit.
2. Straight Flush
Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
3. Four of a Kind
Four cards of identical numerical value.
4. Full House
Three of a kind combined with a pair.
5. Flush
Any five cards of the same suit, not in sequence.
6. Straight
Five sequential cards of mixed suits.
7. Three of a Kind (Trips)
Three cards matching the same numerical value.
8. Two Pair
Two distinct numerical pairs.
9. One Pair
Two cards matching the same numerical value.
10. High Card
No hands match; highest rank card wins.
Tiebreakers & Details
Do Suits Matter for Ties?
No. All suits are equal. If hands match completely, the pot is split evenly.
Can an Ace Start a Low Straight?
Yes! An Ace can act as the lowest card to create an A-2-3-4-5 straight.
What is a "Kicker" Tiebreaker?
If hand strengths tie (e.g. same pair), the game compares remaining highest unused hole or community cards. High kicker wins.
